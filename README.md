## Project Overview

This project converts a raw OPay bank statement into a fully functional 
financial analytics dashboard. It was built as a portfolio project to 
demonstrate end-to-end data skills: cleaning unstructured data, engineering 
meaningful categories, aggregating patterns, and presenting insights in a 
clear, decision-ready format.

No expensive tools. No organizational dataset. Just a mobile banking 
statement, Excel, and structured thinking.

## Key Findings

**1. The wallet operates as a transit account, not a store of value.**
From May 2025 onward, monthly net cash flow was consistently ₦0 — money 
flows through the account rather than accumulating in it. Financial stability 
depends entirely on the consistency of inflows.

**2. Social transfers dominate spending.**
53.9% of all debits were outward transfers to other people — the single 
largest spending category, ahead of POS, bills, and airtime combined. In 
Nigeria, a mobile wallet, holds more than personal funds. It holds community 
obligations.

**3. Intentional fund distribution across accounts.**
Over ₦1 million was transferred to the account holder's own external bank 
accounts — evidence of deliberate multi-account money management rather than 
uncontrolled outflow.

**4. Peak activity months reveal distinct financial events.**
- **May 2025** — highest volume month at ₦1.54M, suggesting a significant 
income event
- **June 2025** — most active month at 248 transactions (~8/day), indicating 
intense financial activity
- **December 2025** — second highest volume at ₦993K, consistent with 
seasonal spending patterns

**5. Savings habit formed and sustained.**
The OWealth account, opened in April 2025, was maintained consistently 
across all 14 months — closing at ₦31,514.84 with over ₦3,172 earned in 
passive interest. The habit of starting matters more than the size of the 
opening balance.

## Analyst Note

This project was deliberately built on personal data to make a point: 
Meaningful financial analysis does not require an organizational database, 
a data engineering team, or enterprise software.

A mobile banking statement, a spreadsheet, and a structured approach are 
enough to surface decision-relevant insights.

The same methodology applied here — data structuring, categorization, 
pattern detection, and narrative framing — translates directly to 
organizational, public finance, and policy contexts.
